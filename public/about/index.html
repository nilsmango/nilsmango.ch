About

I am groot... erh Nils Mango! I'm just here so I won't get fined, plus to make things and try to eliminate the resulting errors. Also, I needed a pretty and simple webpage to show off my things. So with the help of Zola here it is, the website. I also will try to build in public and blog about what I have learned etc. as a help to learn better and to write a lot to learn to write better. Which, as you might have noticed, I could gain from.

A (not so) short Timeline

1985-2000 | getting shit done | Breaking the same leg twice, crying, playing a lot, mostly Lego, but also floorball (Unihockey) and in the forests of Muttenz (and learning a lot about Jesus motherfucking Christ, thanks CVJM aka Cevi aka YMCA), going Windsurfing with my father and learning to play the trombone.

2000-2003 | sweet sixteen | I began my apprenticeship to become a chemical laboratory technician1 at Roche2 in Basel. There I became friends with Michael and his brother Tobias, aka Tobinato. They showed me electronic music and an alternative way of living. They gave me a CD with some cracked and open-source music programs with which I started playing around. My very first track (I am a big fan of the ending) was born with the help of one of these programs and my first digital audio workstation (I sadly can't remember what it was called). Bought my first hardware synth, a Roland SH-32 (I loved that arpeggiator to death), and made some more music with a friend under the name Megaduo. Ironically only two tracks made it to the future: 10 and Morgenflug.
After my apprenticeship, I wanted to join Greenpeace because I wished to save the world3.

2003-2004 | Maturing? | Got my "Berufsmatura" while getting paid by Roche. Thank you! After a strange night out, I drew the first version of the "Zeichen" - the official Logo of Nils Mango. Also won the Swiss Unihockey Championship U18 Elite with Basel Magic, I also drank a lot there. Stopped playing shortly after because I didn't like the new trainer and liked partying way more.

2004 | Bildungsreise | Started taking pictures and traveled to Nepal and a few months later through India, Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, and back again, just making it back before the tsunami hit because my mother wanted me home for Christmas. During traveling, I discovered the music of the Beatles and learned many lessons for life. 2013 Grew a beard. Performed my first live set, and had my first paid gig. Changed my Artist name to Nils Mango6.
I made some things with 3D printing and started Project ReUse (wow those printers were expensive in the beginning). Went surfing in Spain.

I changed the name of chemicalshirts to Molecule Store because I started to sell not only science & chemistry t-shirts but also 3D printed molecule jewelry. The Molecule Store is still going strong today and we now sell over 300 science-themed products with a beautiful twist: You get to shop nerdy AND do good. 1 USD for each product you order gets donated to science education.
